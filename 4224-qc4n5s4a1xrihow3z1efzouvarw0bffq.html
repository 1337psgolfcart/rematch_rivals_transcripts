The initial time was 1 second and 869 milliseconds.
batik of 「 ✦ Right Click ✦ 」 then proceeds to run out of "the box" area.
Accounting for the perspective, we say that batik of 「 ✦ Right Click ✦ 」 leaving the area took 834 milliseconds.
batik of 「 ✦ Right Click ✦ 」 leaving the area makes him the "restricted" player, that is not allowed to enter the box.
Bunny of 「 ✦ Right Click ✦ 」 left "the box" area as well, making it 3 players in the box area.
batik of 「 ✦ Right Click ✦ 」 proceeds to enter "the box" area again, making it 4 players.

「 ✦ Fenix Esports ✦ 」 argued, that there were more than 4 players in the box and the "fourth player" actively interfered in the play.

The Facts:
- 「 ✦ Right Click ✦ 」 did not have 4 on-field players and 1 goalkeeper in the box for longer than 3 seconds
-# The times mentioned are single instances. In neither were 3 seconds overstepped.
- batik of 「 ✦ Right Click ✦ 」 entered "the box" area while still being the "restricted" player.

The Rule breach:
When the ball is in your half and the opponent holds possession, a maximum of 4 defensive players (1 Goalkeeper + 3 Field Players) are permitted within the penalty area.
If more than 3 field players occupy the area, the extra player
must exit within 3 seconds.
The “restricted” defender positioned outside the area is prohibited
from re-entering until:
1. Possession is clearly regained by your team.
or
2. The ball crosses into the opponent's half.
The important part, of why the rule has been broken, is marked in bold.
Rule 3.3 was broken, due to batik of 「 ✦ Right Click ✦ 」 being the "restricted" player in the scenario and re-entering "the box" area.
The Scenario does not get reset, due to the team not gaining clear possession outside of "the box" area, as there are still 5 players in that area after, OR the ball not passing over the middle line.
In this case the rule wasn't broken due to the typical "there are 4 players in the box" but due to the restricted player entering the area.
The original argument of 「 ✦ Fenix Esports ✦ 」 wasn't the point in which the decision was made, as that's addressed with the first fact of "The Facts".
